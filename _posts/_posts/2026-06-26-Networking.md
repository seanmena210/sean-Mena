# Networking 

**Posted on: 6/26/26**

Documented today's work: tested and tagged Ethernet cables, assisted users with account lockout,
and coordinated with IT admin for resolution.


Today I was able to work with Ethernet wires, testing and tagging how many feet the lines were.
I got in super early and was able to meet some cool people from other teams. They came into the 
IT office for assistance, but I didn’t have my permissions set up quite yet. We were able to talk 
about the issue occurring, which was most likely due to a password lock. Once the admin came in, 
we were able to unlock the end user. They were so cool—they even brought me a USAA cowboy hat. 
Later that day, after lunch, I was able to test the Ethernet cables. 
It’s one thing to do it in class in a lab, but another to actually be doing it in the field.

### Daily Log – Networking & IT Support

- Arrived early and had the chance to meet team members from other departments.
- Assisted with an end‑user account issue; identified a likely password lockout and coordinated with the admin to resolve it.
- Tested and tagged Ethernet cables, measuring cable lengths and verifying functionality.
- Gained hands‑on field experience with Ethernet wiring—very different from classroom lab work.
- Bonus: received a USAA cowboy hat !!!
